1. SOLID is an acronym for the first five object-oriented design (OOD) principles by
   Robert C. Martin (also known as Uncle Bob).
   
2. These principles establish practices that lend to developing software with considerations for maintaining and extending as the project grows.
   Adopting these practices can also contribute to avoiding code smells, refactoring code, and Agile or Adaptive software development.

3. SOLID stands for:

   S - Single-responsibility Principle
   O - Open-closed Principle
   L - Liskov Substitution Principle
   I - Interface Segregation Principle
   D - Dependency Inversion Principle
